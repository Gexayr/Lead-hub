# Design System Strategy: The Architectural Intelligence

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**The Creative North Star: "The Obsidian Architect"**

For a B2B sales tool, trust isn't built with generic blue boxes; it’s built through precision, intentionality, and a sense of architectural permanence. This design system moves away from the "standard SaaS template" by embracing **The Obsidian Architect**—a philosophy where the UI feels carved from a single digital monolith. 

We achieve this through **Intentional Asymmetry** (e.g., placing a high-density data visualization against a vast, airy headline) and **Tonal Depth**. By utilizing the Material Design surface tiers as physical layers rather than flat HEX codes, we create a "Tech-Forward" environment that feels like a high-end physical workspace. The interface doesn't just display data; it curates it.

## 2. Colors & Surface Philosophy
The palette is rooted in deep midnight tones (`#0b1326`), utilizing cool secondary blues to provide a sense of professional calm.

### The "No-Line" Rule
To achieve a premium editorial feel, **1px solid borders for sectioning are strictly prohibited.** 
*   **The Law:** Boundaries must be defined solely through background color shifts.
*   **Implementation:** Transition from a `surface` background to a `surface-container-low` section to denote a change in content. This creates a "soft-edge" layout that feels modern and fluid.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a series of stacked sheets of fine material. 
*   **Base Layer:** `surface` (#0b1326).
*   **Section Layer:** `surface-container-low` (#131b2e) for secondary content blocks.
*   **Component Layer:** `surface-container-high` (#222a3d) for interactive cards.
*   **Floating Layer:** `surface-bright` (#31394d) for active states or modals.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
Flat colors lack "soul." Main CTAs and hero headers should utilize subtle linear gradients:
*   **Signature Gradient:** `primary` (#b3c5ff) to `primary-container` (#005be6) at a 135° angle.
*   **Glassmorphism:** For floating navigation or tooltips, use `surface-container-highest` at 70% opacity with a `20px` backdrop-blur. This allows the primary accents to bleed through, softening the tech-heavy aesthetic.

## 3. Typography: The Editorial Edge
We pair the utilitarian precision of **Inter** with the structural elegance of **Manrope**.

*   **Display & Headlines (Manrope):** Large-scale headers (using `display-lg` at 3.5rem) should be used with tight letter-spacing (-0.02em) to create a "Commanding" presence. This reflects the "Efficiency" aspect of the tool.
*   **Body & Titles (Inter):** All functional text uses Inter. Title scales (`title-lg`) should be medium weight to provide a clear contrast against the Manrope headers.
*   **Labels (Inter):** Small labels (`label-sm`) should always be uppercase with +0.05em tracking to evoke a "Technical/Data" aesthetic.

## 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering
Traditional drop shadows are too "web 2.0" for this system. We use light, not shadow, to define space.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Instead of a shadow, place a `surface-container-lowest` (#060e20) card inside a `surface-container` (#171f33) section. The natural contrast creates an "inset" look that feels premium.
*   **Ambient Shadows:** If an element must float (e.g., a dropdown), use a shadow color derived from `on-surface` at 6% opacity with a `40px` blur and `10px` Y-offset.
*   **The Ghost Border:** If accessibility requires a stroke, use `outline-variant` (#434656) at **15% opacity**. It should be felt, not seen.

## 5. Components & Interface Elements

### Buttons
*   **Primary:** Gradient fill (`primary` to `primary-container`), white text (`on-primary`), and `xl` (0.75rem) roundedness. 
*   **Secondary:** `surface-container-highest` fill with no border. 
*   **Interaction:** On hover, increase the gradient intensity; do not use a standard "darken" filter.

### Cards & Lists
*   **The "Divider-Free" Rule:** Lists and card items must never use horizontal lines. Use the **Spacing Scale** (specifically `spacing-4` or `spacing-6`) to create separation.
*   **Surface Shift:** Active list items should shift from `surface` to `surface-container-low`.

### Input Fields
*   **Styling:** Fill with `surface-container-lowest`. Use a "Ghost Border" that transitions to a `primary` 1px border only on `:focus`.
*   **Labels:** Use `label-md` in `on-surface-variant` colors, positioned 0.5rem above the input.

### Data Chips
*   Used for status (e.g., "Lead Qualified"). Use `secondary-container` backgrounds with `on-secondary-container` text. Keep corners `full` (9999px) for a modern, tech-forward pill shape.

### Signature Component: The "Lead Insight" Card
A specialized card for this B2B tool using `surface-container-high`, featuring a left-accent border of `tertiary` (#ffb4a1) to draw the eye to high-priority sales data without cluttering the UI.

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
*   **Do** use asymmetrical margins (e.g., a 2/3 grid layout) to create a high-end editorial feel.
*   **Do** use `surface-tint` at low opacities (2-4%) as an overlay for hero sections to unify the color story.
*   **Do** prioritize "Breathing Room" using `spacing-16` and `spacing-24` for section padding.

### Don't:
*   **Don't** use 100% opaque `outline` colors for borders.
*   **Don't** use standard "Blue" links; use `primary-fixed-dim` with a 1px underline offset by 2px.
*   **Don't** use sharp `none` corners; always maintain at least `DEFAULT` (0.25rem) to keep the "Modern" softness.
*   **Don't** clutter the screen with dividers. If the hierarchy is confusing without lines, your surface tonal shifts are not distinct enough.
